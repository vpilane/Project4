Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

OSHA Requirements

  • Rubber gloves
  • Face shields
  • Safety shoes or boots
  • Insulating sleeves
  • Flame-resistant clothing

Additional Insulating Protective Equipment (IPE)

  • Insulating line hoses and hoods
  • Insulating barriers
  • Live-line tools like shotgun sticks

1926.416(a)(3) Circuit Safety

Ensure circuits are properly labeled and inspected. Workers should be trained in identifying live circuits and proper shutdown procedures.

Electrical Hazards

Common Electrical Hazards

  • Electric shock
  • Arc burns
  • Electrical fires

Injury can occur from direct electrical burns or indirect injuries such as falls or muscle contractions.

Prevention

  • Ensure proper insulation of wires
  • Use non-conductive tools
  • Follow the Lock Out Tag Out (LOTO) system
  • Use GFCIs in high-risk areas
General Safety Tips

Safety Tips for Working with Electricity

  • Make a plan before starting any work
  • Wear proper clothing and PPE
  • Inspect tools and equipment before use
  • Use a buddy system

Additional Guidelines

  • Avoid working in wet areas
  • Keep a minimum distance from overhead power lines
  • Test power cords regularly for damage
Emergency Procedures

Steps in Case of Electrical Injury

  • Call an ambulance immediately
  • Use a non-conductive item to remove the victim from the circuit if necessary
  • Administer CPR if the individual is not breathing

Handling Electrical Accidents

Ensure the power source is cut off first. If you can't do so immediately, use a dry, non-conductive material to safely remove the individual from the source.
